Mutabbaq

Description

Mutabbaq is a type of stuffed pastry that's popular in Saudi Arabia.
It's usually filled with spiced meat (such as minced lamb or chicken) and onions, folded, and then fried until crispy

Ingredients

  • Pastry dough (flour, water, oil)
  • Meat filling (spiced lamb or chicken, onions)

How to make it

  1. Prepare the pastry dough by mixing flour, water, salt, and oil until smooth. Let it rest for about 30 minutes

  2. Divide the dough into small balls and roll each ball into thin circles

  3. Spread a mixture of spiced minced meat (usually lamb or chicken) and finely chopped onions evenly over half of each circle

  4. Fold the dough over the filling to form a half-moon shape, pressing the edges firmly to seal

  5. Heat a skillet or griddle over medium heat, brush the surface with oil, and cook the mutabbaq on both sides until golden and crispy

  6. Serve hot, optionally with a side of yogurt or a dipping sauce
