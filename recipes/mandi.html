Mandi

Description

Mandi is a traditional Saudi Arabian dish of rice, meat (usually lamp or chicken), and a mixture of spices.
The meat is typically slow-cooked in a tandoor (underground clay oven), which gives it a distinctive smoky flavor

Ingredients

  • Rice (typically basmati rice)
  • Meat (usually lamb or chicken)

How to make it

  1. Clean and rinse the rice (usually basmati rice) thoroughly until the water runs clear

  2. Rub the meat (lamb or chicken) with a mixture of spices like black pepper, cardamom, cloves, cinnamon, and salt

  3. In a large pot or tandoor (clay oven), layer the marinated meat at the bottom

  4. Spread the rinsed rice evenly over the meat

  5. Add water (usually in a ratio of 2:1 rice to water), cover the pot, and cook over low heat or in the tandoor until the rice and meat are tender and cooked through

  6. Serve hot, garnished with fried nuts and raisins, and accompanied by yogurt or a spicy tomato sauce
